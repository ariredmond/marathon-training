Coaching Guidelines
How to Execute This Plan

Jack Daniels' method works because of discipline on easy days as much as effort on hard days. Read these before the plan starts.

Jack Daniels Easy Pace Rule

Easy runs must be genuinely easy — 8:30 to 9:15/mi. This isn't a suggestion. Daniels defines easy as 59–74% of max heart rate. If you run Monday's easy run at 8:00/mi because you feel good, you are stealing recovery from Wednesday's workout. The easy days are where adaptation happens — not the hard ones.

The Thursday Rule

On 6-day weeks, Thursday is always 8:45–9:15/mi with no exceptions. It sits between Wednesday's quality session and Friday's easy run. Its only job is to add volume without stress. If Thursday ever feels like a workout, you're running it too fast. Treat it as active recovery.

Two Quality Days

From Week 9 onward, Wednesday workout + Saturday MP long run = 2 quality days. Your V.O2 plan already trained you for this pattern. The key is that the days between (Thursday, Friday) must be easy enough to allow Wednesday's quality to be fully absorbed before Saturday's quality session. Never compress recovery between quality days.

Long Run MP Miles

The 6-mile MP finish structure (miles 15–20 at 7:04) is Daniels M-pace work at its most effective — goal pace on tired legs. Never start the MP block early. Run miles 1–14 genuinely easy even if you feel great. The adaptation happens specifically in that fatigued state. Fuel every 35–40 minutes throughout.

Cutback Weeks

Weeks 6 and 11 are non-negotiable down weeks. Do not make them up. Fitness is not lost in one low week — it's absorbed. Skipping a cutback week doesn't accelerate the plan; it compounds fatigue and delays adaptation. Week 11 is especially important because it follows a race.

Tune-Up Half: Aug 23

Target 1:28–1:30. Start miles 1–4 at 6:52–6:55 no matter how good you feel. Build through miles 5–9. Press in the final 5K. A sub-1:29 half confirms 3:05 marathon shape. The result recalibrates your training paces for the final 8 weeks — if you run 1:26, your easy pace stays the same but your MP drops to ~7:00.

Taper Madness

In Weeks 16–18 you will feel sluggish, anxious, and convinced you've lost fitness. Your legs may feel heavy and dead. This is normal — glycogen stores are replenishing and muscle damage is repairing. The restlessness is the taper working. Stick to prescribed miles exactly. Do not add extra runs.

Race Day Execution

Miles 1–6 at 7:08–7:10/mi. Miles 7–13 at 7:05–7:08. Miles 14–20 at 7:02–7:06. Miles 21–26.2: race. First half should clock ~1:33–1:34. A runner who goes out at 7:00 and blows up at mile 20 runs 3:20. A runner who goes out at 7:08 and finishes strong runs 3:04. Patience in the first 10K is the race.
